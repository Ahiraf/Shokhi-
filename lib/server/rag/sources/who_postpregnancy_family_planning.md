## What postpregnancy family planning means
Postpregnancy family planning is the use of contraception after a pregnancy ends — either after childbirth (postpartum family planning) or after an abortion (post-abortion family planning) — by a woman who wants to avoid an unintended or closely spaced pregnancy. Spacing pregnancies gives the mother's body time to recover and is safer for both mother and baby.

## Why it matters
The time around childbirth and after an abortion is an important opportunity to offer a woman information and a method of her choice, because she is often in contact with health services. Access to postpregnancy family planning helps countries make progress toward the health goal (SDG 3) and gender-equality goal (SDG 5), and supports a woman's right to decide whether and when to have children.

## Quality and choice
Good postpregnancy family-planning services offer counselling, a full range of methods, and respect for the woman's own decision, with attention to quality of care and gender equality. A woman can talk with a health worker before or soon after delivery about which method suits her, including methods that are safe while breastfeeding. This is general information; a doctor or family-planning worker can help her choose what is right for her personally.
