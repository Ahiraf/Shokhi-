## What PCOS is
Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal condition in which the body has higher-than-normal levels of androgens. It affects an estimated 10–13% of women of reproductive age, and up to 70% of those affected remain undiagnosed.

## Common signs
Women with PCOS may have irregular or infrequent menstrual periods, difficulty getting pregnant, excess facial or body hair, hair loss, and acne. Symptoms vary a lot from person to person. PCOS is the most common cause of anovulation (no egg release) and a leading cause of infertility.

## Diagnosis and management
PCOS is diagnosed by a health provider using menstrual history, blood tests for androgen levels, and sometimes an ultrasound of the ovaries — it cannot be confirmed from symptoms alone. Healthy lifestyle behaviours, including healthy eating and physical activity, are important for all women with PCOS. Medicines such as combined oral contraceptives can help regulate periods and reduce symptoms, and fertility treatment can help with conception. There is no cure, but symptoms can be managed. PCOS is also linked with a higher risk of insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes and heart disease, so regular check-ups help.
