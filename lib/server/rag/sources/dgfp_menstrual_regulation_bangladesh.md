## What menstrual regulation (MR) is
In Bangladesh, menstrual regulation (MR) is a government-provided service to bring back menstruation and return a woman to a non-pregnant state after a missed period. It is offered through the Directorate General of Family Planning (DGFP). Depending on the method and provider, MR is generally available up to about 10–12 weeks after the last menstrual period; the medication method (MRM, using mifepristone and misoprostol) is used up to about 9 weeks.

## Getting it safely
MR should be done by a trained provider at a recognised government or approved facility — not with unknown pills bought without advice, which can be unsafe. Trained providers use safe methods (manual vacuum aspiration or approved medication) and can advise on what is right for the woman's situation. After MR, it is important to start a family-planning method to avoid another unintended pregnancy, and services can provide this counselling.

## Post-abortion care and when to seek help
If a woman has heavy bleeding, severe pain, fever, or bad-smelling discharge after a miscarriage or MR, she should seek care immediately — post-abortion care is available at DGHS facilities and can treat complications. In an emergency call 999; for free, confidential advice call the health hotline Shastho Batayon on 16263. The guidance here is general information, not a substitute for seeing a trained health provider.
