## What PCOS is
Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal condition affecting women of reproductive age. It is a leading cause of irregular periods and difficulty getting pregnant, but it can be managed.

## Common signs
Signs may include irregular or missed periods, extra hair on the face or body, acne, weight gain, and trouble conceiving. Not every woman has every sign. PCOS is diagnosed by a doctor, often with an examination, blood tests, and sometimes an ultrasound — it cannot be confirmed from symptoms alone.

## Managing PCOS
Healthy lifestyle steps are usually the first-line help: a balanced diet, regular physical activity, and reaching or keeping a healthy weight can improve periods and symptoms. A doctor may also suggest specific treatments depending on whether a woman wants to manage symptoms or become pregnant. PCOS is also linked with a higher long-term risk of type 2 diabetes, so regular check-ups help.
