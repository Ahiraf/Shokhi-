## What endometriosis is
Endometriosis is a chronic condition affecting roughly 10% of women and girls of reproductive age worldwide. Tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows outside the uterus, causing inflammation and scarring.

## Symptoms and impact
Many women have severe pain during menstruation and heavy bleeding. The condition often causes chronic pelvic pain that continues beyond the period, and pain during sex, bowel movements or urination. Up to half of women who struggle to conceive may have endometriosis. The pain can be debilitating enough to prevent work or school, and it is linked with fatigue, depression and anxiety.

## Diagnosis, treatment and when to seek care
There is currently no cure, but symptoms can respond to pain-relief medicines (NSAIDs), hormonal contraceptives, and sometimes surgery. Diagnosis is often delayed by several years because symptoms vary and awareness is low. See a doctor for severe period pain, chronic pelvic pain, painful intercourse, or difficulty getting pregnant. Early diagnosis and care that combines medication, therapy and support can greatly improve quality of life.
