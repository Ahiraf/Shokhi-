## What this guidance is
The WHO Medical eligibility criteria for contraceptive use (MEC), sixth edition, gives evidence-based recommendations on the safety of different contraceptive methods for people who have specific health conditions or characteristics. Its purpose is to help health workers support a woman to choose and safely use a family-planning method.

## How safety is decided
For each medical condition, a method is placed into one of four categories. Categories 1 and 2 mean the woman can generally use the method (the benefits usually outweigh any risk). Categories 3 and 4 mean the method should generally not be used, because the risk usually outweighs the benefit. Safety considers whether a method could worsen a condition or add health risks, and whether a condition makes the method less effective — always weighed against the benefit of preventing an unintended pregnancy.

## Methods covered
The guidance covers a wide range of methods, including low-dose combined oral contraceptives, the patch and vaginal ring, injectables (DMPA, NET-EN), progestogen-only pills, implants, emergency contraceptive pills, and copper and hormonal (levonorgestrel) intrauterine devices (IUDs). A woman should discuss her health history with a doctor or family-planning worker to find the method that is safe and right for her.
