## Free services, close to home
In Bangladesh the government provides family-planning information and contraceptives **free** through the Directorate General of Family Planning (DGFP). Field workers (family welfare assistants) and Union and Upazila family-planning offices bring services close to where women live. A woman can get counselling to choose the method that suits her, and services are confidential.

## Methods available
The national program offers a range of methods: the combined oral contraceptive pill (the national brand is called "Sukhi"), progestogen-only pills, injectable contraceptives, intrauterine devices (IUDs), implants, condoms, emergency contraception, and permanent methods (female or male sterilisation) for those who have completed their family. Which method is safe depends on a woman's health, so it is best chosen with a health worker.

## After childbirth, and where to ask
The program gives special attention to family planning right after childbirth (postpartum family planning), which helps a mother space her pregnancies safely. About 12% of married women in Bangladesh still have an unmet need for family planning. To start, a woman can visit her nearest community clinic, union health & family welfare centre, or upazila health complex, or call the government health hotline Shastho Batayon on 16263 — the guidance is free and non-judgmental.
