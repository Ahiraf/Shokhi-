## Why iron matters in pregnancy
Anaemia (low blood / কম রক্ত) is common in pregnancy and can make a woman feel very tired, weak, or dizzy. Severe anaemia raises the risk of problems during delivery.

## Recommended supplementation
WHO recommends daily oral iron and folic acid supplementation for pregnant women to prevent maternal anaemia. A commonly recommended dose is 30–60 mg of elemental iron together with 400 micrograms (0.4 mg) of folic acid daily, throughout pregnancy, starting as early as possible.

## Food and follow-up
Iron-rich foods (green leafy vegetables, liver, meat, beans, dates) help alongside the supplement. Vitamin C foods (lemon, guava) help the body absorb iron. A woman who feels very weak, breathless, or dizzy should see a doctor or health worker, who may check her blood (haemoglobin) level.
