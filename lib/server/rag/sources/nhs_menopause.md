## What menopause and perimenopause are
Menopause is when periods stop for good because hormone levels fall; perimenopause is the time before, when symptoms have started but periods have not yet fully stopped. They are a natural part of life and usually affect women between the ages of 45 and 55, though they can happen earlier.

## Common symptoms
Periods often become irregular before stopping. Many women get hot flushes and night sweats — a sudden feeling of heat in the face, neck and chest, sometimes with sweating, palpitations or dizziness lasting a few minutes. Mental-health symptoms are common too: mood swings, low mood or anxiety, and problems with memory or concentration ("brain fog"), often made worse by poor sleep. Physical symptoms can include vaginal dryness and discomfort during sex, reduced sex drive, more frequent urinary infections and needing to pass urine more often, palpitations, headaches, joint and muscle aches, and changes to skin and hair.

## When to see a doctor
Contact a doctor if you think you have perimenopause or menopause symptoms, especially if your bleeding pattern changes a lot, or if you have any vaginal bleeding after 12 months without a period. Treatments and self-care can reduce the impact on health, relationships and work.
