## How many check-ups, and when
Antenatal care (ANC) means the health check-ups a woman should have during pregnancy. Bangladesh's national schedule recommends at least four ANC visits: the first before about 16 weeks, the second between 24 and 28 weeks, the third around 32 weeks, and the fourth around 36 weeks. WHO now recommends at least eight contacts for the best care. Starting early and going to every visit helps keep the mother and baby safe.

## What happens at a visit, and where
At each visit a health worker checks blood pressure, weight and (when needed) blood and urine; gives the tetanus (TT/Td) vaccine and iron–folic acid tablets to prevent anaemia; and answers questions. These services are available free at government facilities — community clinics, union health & family welfare centres, and upazila health complexes.

## Danger signs — go to a facility at once
During pregnancy, seek care immediately for: vaginal bleeding, severe headache, blurred vision, swelling of the face or hands, convulsions (fits), high fever, or the baby moving much less than usual. In an emergency call 999; for free health advice call the government health hotline Shastho Batayon 16263.
