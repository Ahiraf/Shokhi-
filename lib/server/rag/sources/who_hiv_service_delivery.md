## Purpose
The WHO Consolidated HIV guidelines on service delivery bring together WHO's recommendations on how HIV services should be organized, so that care is more people-centred, integrated and fair, and so more people can get tested, prevented and treated.

## People-centred, integrated services
The guidelines support delivering HIV testing, prevention and treatment in ways that fit people's lives — closer to the community, with less stigma, and joined up with other health services a woman may already use, such as antenatal (pregnancy) care, family planning and sexual-health services. Integrating HIV services with maternal and reproductive health helps reach women and prevent mother-to-child transmission.

## Access and continuity of care
A central goal is to improve access, keep people in care over time (continuity of care), and reduce the steps and barriers between testing and treatment. HIV testing is confidential, and effective treatment allows a person living with HIV to stay healthy and prevents passing the virus to others. This is general information; a woman with concerns about HIV should speak with a health worker or visit a testing and treatment service, where support is confidential and non-judgmental.
