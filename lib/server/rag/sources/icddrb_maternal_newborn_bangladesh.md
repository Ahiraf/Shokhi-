## Preventing bleeding after birth
Bleeding after childbirth (postpartum haemorrhage) is a leading cause of maternal death. icddr,b research showed that giving the medicine misoprostol through clean delivery kits protected about 60% of home births in the project from postpartum haemorrhage — evidence that has helped shape safer delivery practice in Bangladesh. A skilled birth attendant and delivering at a facility make birth safer for mother and baby.

## Community health workers reach women at home
Since the late 1970s, icddr,b pioneered a model using large numbers of trained female community health workers to bring family planning and maternal care to women's doorsteps. This dramatically increased contraceptive use and lowered fertility, and the approach is now used widely across Bangladesh — showing that trusted local workers can reach rural women who cannot easily travel.

## Trusted information for adolescents
icddr,b worked with adolescents to build a bank of reproductive-health questions and answers, used to update a Bangla reproductive-health booklet series called "Nijeke Jano" ("Know Yourself"). Giving young people clear, non-judgmental, science-based answers in Bangla helps counter myths and shame. This is general information; a woman or girl with a health concern should also speak with a doctor or health worker.
